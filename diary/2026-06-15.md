**CUDA texture x/y permutation — confirmed false positive:**
Traced through the CUDA 3D texture setup: `extent.width=NY, extent.height=NX`. In `tex3D(tex, u, v, w)`, `u` addresses width (NY/j-dim) and `v` addresses height (NX/i-dim). The call `tex3D(tex, (worldY+1)*0.5, (worldX+1)*0.5, ...)` correctly maps worldY→j and worldX→i. The two swaps (in extent and in tex3D args) cancel exactly. Correct for any NX, NY.

## Lessons
- zerolog v1.32.0 has no `ExitFunc` variable — the only way to intercept `log.Fatal` before `os.Exit(1)` is a zerolog hook; hooks are called in `Event.write()` before the `done` callback, so panicking in a hook prevents `os.Exit` from ever being reached.
- VoxelGrid.Density() uses `(N-1)` scaling (world x=1 → voxel N-1), but the render loop and ExportToRaw use `i/N` convention (world x=1-2/N at i=N-1). Tests that mix conventions will produce subtle off-by-one failures that look like interpolation errors.
- For non-cubic grids, NZ=4 gives z-voxels at ±1/3 — a sphere of radius 0.2 centered at z=0 has no voxels inside it. Always check that at least 2–3 voxel centres land inside any sphere used in a test.
- Two adversarial "real bug" findings were both false positives. The right-endpoint rule insight is non-obvious: the skipped `old_left` in the hierarchical integrator is intentional and correct, not missing.
